# Ch 12 · Assessing Liver Fibrosis

> The virus is the fuse. Fibrosis is what's actually wrecking the liver.

---

## Why fibrosis matters more than the virus

A counterintuitive fact —

**The virus itself doesn't directly kill liver cells. Your immune system does.**

T cells spot infected cells and attack → infected cells die → liver tissue gets damaged → repeat this for years, and **fibrous tissue grows in** to replace normal liver cells → fibrosis → cirrhosis → liver cancer.

So when you're gauging how serious someone's hepatitis B is, the most important question isn't "how much virus" — it's:

> **How much has your liver already been "remodeled"?**

---

## The four stages of fibrosis

![Fibrosis grading](../assets/fibrosis-zh.svg)

The internationally used **METAVIR score** breaks liver fibrosis into 5 grades:

| Grade | State |
|------|------|
| **F0** | No fibrosis |
| **F1** | Portal tract expansion |
| **F2** | Bridging fibrosis between portal tracts |
| **F3** | Extensive bridging, not yet cirrhosis |
| **F4** | **Cirrhosis** |

**Key thresholds:**

- **F2 is the lower trigger for treatment** — most guidelines recommend starting antivirals at F2 and above
- **F3-F4 needs HCC screening every 3-6 months**
- **F4 (cirrhosis)** — regardless of DNA level, almost always needs treatment

---

## Four ways to assess fibrosis

| Method | Notes |
|------|------|
| **Liver biopsy** | Gold standard, invasive, some bleeding/pain risk |
| **FibroScan (transient elastography)** | Non-invasive, fast, widely used |
| **FIB-4 index** | Calculated from blood work, free |
| **APRI** | Also from blood work, simpler |

---

## FibroScan: the most common non-invasive method

The principle: send a low-frequency vibration wave into the liver and measure **how fast it travels** — the more fibrosis, the stiffer the liver, the faster the wave.

- Result reported in **kPa**
- Takes 5-10 minutes, painless
- **Widely used in China and Europe, available in some Canadian hospitals**

**Reference kPa values for hepatitis B patients (unit: kPa)**:

| Value | METAVIR |
|------|-------------|
| < 6.0 | F0-F1 |
| 6.0 - 7.5 | F1-F2 |
| 7.5 - 9.5 | **F2** (treatment trigger) |
| 9.5 - 12.5 | F3 |
| > 12.5 | **F4 (cirrhosis)** |

**Notes:**

- Do it on an empty stomach — eating raises the reading
- During acute hepatitis or an ALT spike, the reading is falsely high — control inflammation first
- Obesity and ascites hurt accuracy
- Watch the trend across visits, not one reading

---

## FIB-4: a free estimator

**Uses just four routine blood values:**

```
FIB-4 = (age × AST) / (platelets × √ALT)
```

- Units: AST/ALT in U/L, platelets in 10⁹/L, age in years

**Interpretation:**

| FIB-4 | Meaning |
|-------|------|
| < 1.45 | Rules out significant fibrosis |
| 1.45 - 3.25 | **Gray zone** — needs further evaluation (FibroScan or biopsy) |
| > 3.25 | Strongly suggests severe fibrosis / cirrhosis |

**Pros:** no extra tests needed — you only need a standard CBC + liver panel.

**Cons:** less precise than FibroScan; not sensitive for early fibrosis.

---

## APRI: an even simpler estimator

```
APRI = (AST / AST upper limit) / platelets (10⁹/L) × 100
```

**Reference:**

- APRI < 0.5: low probability of fibrosis
- APRI 0.5 - 1.5: moderate
- APRI > 1.5: significant fibrosis
- APRI > 2.0: suggests cirrhosis

**FIB-4 and APRI are both WHO-recommended screening tools for low-resource settings.**

---

## Biopsy: gold standard, but used less and less

Percutaneous liver biopsy used to be the gold standard for assessing fibrosis.

**Pros:**
- Direct look at the histology
- Can also assess inflammation, steatosis, and other pathology

**Cons:**
- Invasive (ultrasound-guided fine-needle)
- Painful
- Bleeding risk (small but real)
- Sampling error (only samples about 1/50000 of the liver)

**Modern practice:**

Most of the time now, **FibroScan + FIB-4 together** are enough to guide treatment. Biopsy is reserved for:

- Conflicting non-invasive results
- Suspected co-existing liver disease (autoimmune, fatty liver, drug-induced)
- Research

---

## Imaging: ultrasound + CT/MRI

FibroScan measures "stiffness." Ultrasound/CT/MRI show you the "structure."

**Imaging cadence for hepatitis B patients:**

| Situation | Frequency |
|------|------|
| Chronic HBV, no cirrhosis | US + AFP every 6-12 months |
| Cirrhosis | US + AFP every 3-6 months, CT/MRI as needed |
| Suspicious nodule on US | Contrast CT or MRI for further evaluation |

**MRI + Gd-EOB-DTPA (Primovist)** is currently the most sensitive tool for diagnosing small HCC lesions, especially nodules < 2 cm.

---

## A combined plan

If you don't have FibroScan available, a practical plan:

1. **Every 6-12 months** do routine blood work and calculate FIB-4
2. **FIB-4 < 1.45** — keep monitoring, do a FibroScan every 3 years
3. **FIB-4 1.45-3.25 or FibroScan > 7.5** — FibroScan yearly
4. **FIB-4 > 3.25 or FibroScan > 12.5** — manage as cirrhosis

---

## Is fibrosis reversible?

Good news: **in the early and middle stages, yes.**

With effective antiviral treatment, fibrosis can **partially reverse** — some F4 patients even come back to F2-F3.

The keys are:

- Virus stays suppressed (DNA undetectable long-term)
- Inflammation calms down (ALT normal long-term)
- Time (at least 3-5 years before you see clear improvement)

**This is why "the earlier you get proper treatment, the more of your liver you can save — or even recover."**

---

## 📍 Key Points

- Fibrosis predicts long-term risk better than the virus itself
- Grading uses **METAVIR F0-F4**; F2 is usually the treatment trigger
- **FibroScan is the standard non-invasive tool**; for HBV the kPa thresholds are 7.5 (F2) and 12.5 (F4)
- **FIB-4 + APRI are free estimators** — only need blood work
- Biopsy is the gold standard but used less and less
- Imaging + AFP is the **HCC screening double-check**
- **Fibrosis is reversible in early/mid stages** — early treatment matters
