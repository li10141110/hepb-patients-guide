# Ch 23 · ASO and Bepirovirsen

> A name that might change the game.

---

## ASO in one minute

ASO = **Antisense Oligonucleotide**.

How it works:

1. You inject a **single-stranded, short DNA analog complementary to HBV mRNA**
2. It gets into the cell and binds the HBV mRNA
3. The cell's **RNase H** recognizes the double-stranded structure and cuts the mRNA
4. HBV proteins can't be made

**Same target as siRNA (mRNA), slightly different mechanism (RNase H instead of RISC).**

---

## The star drug: Bepirovirsen

**Bepirovirsen (GSK)** is currently the most watched HBV drug in the ASO space.

- Subcutaneous injection
- **Once weekly** (more frequent than siRNA)
- Completed Phase II, now in **Phase III (B-Well program)**
- May 2026 GSK released pivotal data: **about 20% of patients achieved functional cure.** A historic moment.

If Phase III data holds, **it could be the first HBV drug explicitly approved with "functional cure" as its indication.**

---

## GSK's clinical milestones

**Bepirovirsen key milestones:**

**Phase 2b (B-Clear, 2022)**:
- 300 mg/week, 24 weeks
- Some participants had HBsAg completely disappear
- Suggested monotherapy could achieve functional cure

**Phase 3 (B-Well, ongoing)**:
- Primary endpoint: sustained HBsAg negativity + sustained DNA negativity 24 weeks after stopping treatment
- 2026 mid-trial data: **about 20%** hit the primary endpoint
- Higher dose group and specific subgroups had better rates

**Data cross-check note: 2026 figures pending confirmation of official final readouts.**

---

## Who's most likely to benefit (based on current data)

**Response features:**

- **Baseline HBsAg < 1000 IU/mL** — strongest predictor
- **HBeAg negative** — better response
- **Relatively young** (< 60 years old)
- **Already stable on NUCs** — better effect

If your HBsAg quantitative is > 10⁴, Bepirovirsen monotherapy may have limited effect. **That's why it's often paired with siRNA or Peg-IFN.**

---

## Current dosing regimen

- **300 mg subcutaneous injection, once weekly**
- 12 or 24 week course
- Combined with background NUC (not stopped)
- Subcutaneous injection usually in the abdomen or thigh

**Side effects:**

- **Injection site reactions** — redness, swelling, itching (most common)
- **Transient ALT elevation** — usually at 4-12 weeks, generally mild
- Rare **platelet drops** — monitoring needed
- Rarely serious adverse events

**Most side effects are manageable without stopping the drug.**

---

## ALT elevation: worry or celebrate?

During Bepirovirsen treatment, some patients see ALT rise. **Don't panic yet.**

Two possibilities:

**① Immune flare** (good sign)
- ALT up + HBsAg down + DNA stable
- Means immunity is moving
- A portion of these patients later achieve HBsAg loss

**② Drug-induced liver injury** (bad sign)
- ALT up + bilirubin up + no HBsAg drop
- Rare, needs stopping and assessment

**Key**: close monitoring + physician judgment. Don't make decisions on your own.

---

## ASO vs siRNA: practical differences

From a patient's perspective:

| Dimension | ASO (Bepirovirsen) | siRNA (VIR-2218 etc.) |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Frequency | **Weekly** | **Every 4-12 weeks** |
| Course | 24 weeks | 24-48 weeks or longer |
| HBsAg drop | Deep, fast | Deep, slightly slower |
| Monotherapy HBsAg loss rate | ~ 10-20% | ~ 5-15% |
| Combination use | Often with NUC + PD-1 | Often with NUC + IFN or vaccine |
| Side effects | Similar | Similar |
| Market progress | **B-Well Phase III, may lead** | Phase II/III rolling out |

**Right now, ASO looks slightly ahead on "monotherapy functional cure" data, and siRNA wins on dosing frequency.**

---

## After Bepirovirsen: ASO's next generations

Bepirovirsen is just the first-generation ASO for HBV. Second-gen designs are moving forward:

- **GalNAc-conjugated ASO** — improves liver targeting, could reduce dosing frequency
- **Stronger silencing efficiency** — lower dose, fewer side effects
- **Sequence optimization** — targeting specific genotypes or integration sites

**If Bepirovirsen gets approved, expect improved versions within a few years.**

## A patient's choice scenario

Suppose in 2028 Bepirovirsen is approved. You're 45, been on TAF for 10 years, HBsAg 800 IU/mL.

Your doctor discusses:

**Option A: Continue TAF alone**
- Stable, no fuss
- But HBsAg drops < 5% per year
- Can't stop treatment

**Option B: Add Bepirovirsen for 24 weeks**
- ~20% chance of functional cure
- ~80% chance HBsAg drops significantly but doesn't fully disappear
- Weekly injections + close monitoring
- Expensive
- ALT may fluctuate

**What do you pick?**

**This is the kind of decision the new-drug era demands. This book aims to help you have the tools to make it.**

---

## 📍 Key Points

- ASO **degrades HBV mRNA via RNase H**. Same target as siRNA, different mechanism
- Star drug **Bepirovirsen (GSK)** already in Phase III
- 2026 data: **about 1 in 5 patients achieved functional cure.** Historic
- Weekly injections, 24-week course
- **Patients with HBsAg < 1000 IU/mL** respond best
- Side effects: injection reactions, ALT fluctuation
- One of the **most likely HBV drugs to be approved in the next 3-5 years**
