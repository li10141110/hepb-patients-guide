# Preface · Why I Wrote This Book

---

I was born a hepatitis B carrier.

My mother passed it to me. She got it from her mother. Three generations, same virus.

As a child, I didn't know what it was. Just a blood test every year, my mother sighing at the report, a doctor saying things I didn't understand, and the reminder: "Don't drink, don't stay up late, come back for checkups."

In college I started reading my own lab reports. I didn't understand them, so I looked them up. And I found something strange —

**The same lab report gets different advice from different doctors. The same number gets different interpretations from different national guidelines.**

I'm trained as an engineer. My first instinct when things don't line up is — **go to the source data**.

So I started reading papers.

From nucleos(t)ide analog clinical trials to cccDNA molecular biology. From vaccine history to the latest CRISPR-HBV preprints. From China's national epidemiology surveys to WHO's global targets.

The more I read, the clearer one thing became —

**Scientists are already talking about "cure," while many patients are still struggling to read a lab report.**

Doctors are busy. The media chases headlines. Serious science communication is rare and often too dense. Patient forums are lively but full of noise.

**The layer in the middle — someone with the background to read the literature and the willingness to translate it into plain language — is missing.**

This book is my attempt to fill a bit of that gap.

---

## I'm Not a Doctor

To be clear — I'm not a physician. I've read a lot of literature and followed many trials, but I can't write your prescription. I can't tell you whether or when to stop your medication.

What I can do is explain complicated things in plain language. I can piece together information scattered across dozens of papers into a map. I can help you understand what your doctor is saying and ask better questions.

**The decision still belongs to you and your doctor.**

---

## Who This Book Is For

- **Newly diagnosed patients** — who want to know what this disease actually is
- **Long-term patients** — who want to know whether new drugs are worth the wait
- **Family members** — who want to understand what a loved one is going through
- **Medical students and researchers** — who want a fast entry into the HBV field
- **Curious readers** — who want to see what a 60-year scientific battle looks like

---

## What This Book Is Not

- **Not a medical textbook** — I don't aim to be comprehensive, only clear on what matters
- **Not clinical practice guidance** — specific treatment decisions belong with your physician
- **Not a promotion of new drugs** — I write about both the hope and the risk
- **Not always right** — medicine changes, data updates, and I make mistakes. Tell me when you find one

---

## Free, Forever

This book is permanently free.

- Read it, download it, print it
- Share it with anyone who needs it
- Translate it into any language
- Cite it in your own work

Just don't: **sell it commercially, or pass it off as your own work.**

If this book helped you, the best thing you can do is — **tell someone else who needs it**.

## One Line

If, twenty years from now, someone can look back and clearly see how humanity cured hepatitis B — and this book is a first-person record of that time, not from a doctor's view, not from a company's view, but from someone who **lived through it and watched it happen** —

that will be enough.
