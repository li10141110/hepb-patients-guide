# Ch 37 · How to Spot Fake News and "Miracle Cures"

> Hepatitis B is a disaster zone for "miracle cures."

---

## An observation

Search "hepatitis B cure" and you'll see dozens of "secret recipes":

- **"Three herbs cleared it in a week"**
- **"XX stem cell therapy can clear the virus"**
- **"Ancestral secret recipe, results in XX days"**
- **"A certain doctor's exclusive formula, never shared"**
- **"Latest American technology, only at XX hospital"**

**99.9% are scams.**

This chapter is about spotting them and protecting yourself.

---

## Six patterns of scams

**① "Ancestral secret recipe"**

- Features: no public formula, no clinical trial, only "legend"
- Common trick: cheap at first, then prices climb
- **Truth: any real drug goes through clinical trials, publishes papers, and registers trademarks**

**② "Latest foreign technology"**

- Features: claims to be already approved in the US/Europe, but you can't find it
- Common trick: "insider channels", "chartered flights to fetch the drug"
- **Truth: all approved drugs can be found on the FDA / EMA / NMPA websites**

**③ "Stem cell therapy"**

- Features: uses stem cells to treat hepatitis B
- **Truth: currently **no stem cell therapy is approved for hepatitis B** — all legitimate clinical trials are very early stage. Any "stem cell cure" charging 100,000+ is a scam**

**④ "Immune modulation" packaging**

- Features: uses "immune modulation" or "boost immunity" as the selling point
- Common trick: some "extract", some "factor"
- **Truth: real immunotherapy is **PD-1 inhibitors, TLR agonists, and other drugs with clinical data** — not supplements**

**⑤ Traditional Chinese medicine "clears the virus"**

- Features: claims to clear HBsAg
- **Truth: a few Chinese medicines may have mild adjunct anti-inflammatory effects, but **no Chinese medicine can clear HBsAg** — modern nucleoside drugs can't do it in 20 years**

**⑥ "Clinical trial" scams**

- Features: asks you to pay to join a "clinical trial"
- Common trick: bed fees, drug fees, import tariffs
- **Truth: legitimate clinical trials **don't charge patients** — usually they provide free drugs and tests, and sometimes even a stipend**

---

## Six "scammer radars"

If you see any of these, be suspicious:

**🚨 Requires big money**
- Legitimate drugs have clear pricing
- Insurance has coverage scope
- **Any request for "a one-time cash payment of 50,000+" — suspect first**

**🚨 Only at a specific hospital/clinic**
- Legitimate drugs are available at many hospitals
- Cure breakthroughs are publicly announced
- **"Only I have it" is usually a scam**

**🚨 Instant results**
- HBV isn't a cold
- **"Works in 3 days", "clears in a week" — all lies**

**🚨 Testimonials but no data**
- Papers have data, trials have controls
- Testimonials can be faked, data can't

**🚨 Tells you to stop your regular meds**
- Real doctors won't tell you to stop TAF/TDF/ETV
- **Any "miracle doctor" who tells you to stop meds is dangerous**

**🚨 No regulatory filing**
- Approved drugs have approval numbers
- "Clinical trials" have registration numbers
- **If you can't find a filing, don't use it**

---

## Three "true scammer" scripts

**"Do you believe in medicine? Do you trust the body's ability to heal itself?"**
- Emotional manipulation
- Purpose: get you to abandon regular treatment

**"Lab results can lie. What your body feels is what's real."**
- Anti-science rhetoric
- Purpose: get you to ignore hospital data

**"This has been passed down in our family / from our master. Regular hospitals won't use it."**
- Mystical packaging
- Purpose: justify a price premium

**When you hear these, refuse outright.**

---

## A scam case (a common pattern)

A patient was promised by an "expert": "HBsAg cleared in 3 months."

**The process:**
1. Add on WeChat, send lab reports
2. "Expert" says: "Your type is our specialty, I'll adjust your regimen"
3. Prescribed "medicine" (decoctions + capsules) monthly, 30,000 yuan
4. After 6 months, HBsAg dropped from 5000 to 4500 (within natural fluctuation range)
5. "Expert" says: "It dropped! That means it's working, keep going!"
6. After a year, spent 400,000 yuan, HBsAg 4200
7. Patient stopped, went back to the hospital, and found elevated ALT — **the "miracle drug" contained hepatotoxic ingredients**

**This isn't fiction. Similar cases are all over the internet.**

## What kinds of "supplements" can be considered

**Cautious but okay:**

- **Coffee** (2-3 cups/day, no sugar) — associated with lower HCC risk
- **Regular exercise** — clearly beneficial
- **Quit smoking, limit alcohol** — clearly beneficial
- **Mediterranean diet** — generally beneficial
- **Counseling** — stress relief is important

**Not recommended:**

- Any Chinese medicine claiming to "clear HBV"
- Any "detox" therapy
- Any unapproved "cell therapy"
- Any regimen that asks you to stop your regular meds

## How to verify whether a drug is real

**Step 1: Check regulators**
- FDA: fda.gov/drugs
- EMA: ema.europa.eu
- NMPA: nmpa.gov.cn
- Enter the drug name, see if there's an approval

**Step 2: Check papers**
- PubMed search
- ClinicalTrials.gov search
- You should be able to find multiple Phase II/III data papers

**Step 3: Check hospitals**
- Top-tier hospital hepatology departments usually prescribe legitimate drugs
- If only "XX clinic" can prescribe it, be wary

**Step 4: Ask your doctor**
- What does your attending doctor think of this drug?
- If your doctor is skeptical, you should be too

**Step 5: Check the price**
- Legitimate drugs have market prices you can look up
- If there's only one channel and the price is outrageous, be wary

## A suggestion: find a "fake-check buddy"

Searching for information on your own may not be objective. **Find a friend who understands medicine or logic, and every time you come across a "miracle cure" claim, let them help you evaluate it**.

- You saw it in a group chat → screenshot it to your friend
- A relative recommends it → let your friend evaluate
- You're worried about missing out → let your friend spell it out

**Two pairs of eyes see more.**

---

## What if you've already been scammed?

**Don't be ashamed.**

First thing: **stop the "miracle drug" immediately**
Second thing: **get a full check-up, especially liver function**
Third thing: **resume regular treatment**
Fourth thing: **if the amount is large, consider a police report or consumer complaint**
Fifth thing: **tell other patients, prevent secondary victims**

**Getting scammed isn't shameful. Not learning from it is.**

---

## One sentence to sum it up

**Scientific breakthroughs in hepatitis B cures will never debut in a WeChat group.**

**Never in a clinic's back room.**

**Never require you to hand over tens of thousands in cash.**

**They will:**

- Publish in NEJM, Lancet, JAMA
- Be announced at international societies
- Get approved at real hospitals
- Have FDA/EMA/NMPA filings
- Have a clear clinical trial number
- Be replicated by multiple independent centers

**That's what "real" looks like.**

---

## 📍 Key Points

- Six common scams: **secret recipes, foreign tech, stem cells, immune modulation, herbal clearance, trial scams**
- Six red-flag radars: **big money, exclusive, instant results, testimonials without data, telling you to stop meds, no filing**
- Three scammer scripts, refuse outright
- Verify a drug: **check regulators → papers → hospitals → doctor → price**
- **Find a fake-check buddy** to help you judge
- If scammed, no shame, cut losses quickly
- **Real breakthroughs always go through legitimate channels**
