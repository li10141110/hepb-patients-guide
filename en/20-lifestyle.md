# Ch 20 · Lifestyle

> Drugs suppress the virus. You have to take care of the liver itself.

---

One-line principle:

> **Besides the virus, take every liver-harming thing seriously.**

Because a liver already dealing with chronic inflammation can't handle a second hit.

---

## Alcohol: better not to drink

The most honest answer about drinking — **if you can not drink, don't**.

- Chronic HBV + alcohol significantly raises cirrhosis risk
- More than 30 g of alcohol per day (equal to 500 ml beer / 150 ml wine / 50 ml spirits) clearly raises risk
- **HBV + alcohol + fatty liver is a "trio of death"**

**If you really must drink:**
- Less than 3 times a week
- Less than 20 g of alcohol each time (half a beer or a small glass of wine)
- **Cirrhosis patients: not a drop**

---

## Diet: not "avoiding foods," but "balance"

Many patients are told to "avoid spicy food, seafood, and eggs" — **most of that has no scientific basis**.

**What actually matters:**

**① Avoid fatty liver**
- Fatty liver + HBV = doubled liver cancer risk
- Control total calories, cut refined sugar and refined carbs
- Cut back on sugary drinks

**② Enough high-quality protein**
- Eggs, fish, soy products, lean meat are all fine
- Cirrhosis patients adjust protein intake per doctor's advice

**③ Be careful with supplements and "liver-protecting drugs"**
- **Sketchy traditional herbs and supplements are the number one source of drug-induced liver injury**
- He Shou Wu, Tu San Qi, Lei Gong Teng all have documented liver toxicity reports
- **"Liver-protecting pills" can't replace standard antiviral therapy**

**④ Coffee is good**
- Multiple studies link coffee with lower risk of cirrhosis and liver cancer
- 2–3 cups of unsweetened coffee a day is fine

**⑤ Watch for aflatoxin**
- Moldy nuts, corn, peanuts — **absolutely don't eat them**
- Aflatoxin + HBV = the classic liver cancer combination

---

## Exercise: move when you can

Some patients are trapped by the old "must rest" mindset and haven't exercised in decades. **That's wrong.**

**Modern advice:**

- Aerobic + strength training, more than 150 minutes a week
- Intensity: "can speak but can't sing"
- **Except during an acute hepatitis flare** (extremely high ALT), when hold off on vigorous exercise

**Benefits of exercise:**
- Improves fatty liver (common HBV comorbidity)
- Improves metabolism
- Mental health (chronic disease patients have higher depression risk)

**Avoid:**
- High-impact contact sports (e.g., in cirrhosis + splenomegaly, avoid rugby, etc.)
- Extreme dehydration (hydrate well for marathons)

**Bottom line: run when you can, train when you can. Don't use the disease as an excuse to sit it out.**

---

## Sleep: it really matters

The liver repairs at night. Chronic night-owl patients see:

- ALT more likely to rise
- Poorer immune response
- Worse metabolic disturbance

**Recommendations:**
- Try to sleep before 11 pm
- 7–8 hours a night
- **Sleep is a free "liver protector"**

---

## Vaccines: get the ones you should

Vaccines chronic HBV patients should complete:

- **Hepatitis A vaccine** — hep A layered on hep B can cause severe hepatitis
- **Flu vaccine** — yearly
- **Pneumococcal vaccine** — especially for cirrhosis
- **COVID vaccine** — per local recommendations
- **HPV vaccine** — for eligible women
- **Shingles vaccine** — over 50
- **No HDV vaccine** — but the HBV vaccine indirectly prevents HDV

**Avoid:**
- **Live vaccines** — use caution in decompensated cirrhosis or immunosuppression

---

## Medications and supplements: careful and cautious

**Before taking any drug long-term that a doctor didn't prescribe, ask your doctor.**

**Watch out for:**
- **Acetaminophen** (Tylenol, Panadol) — safe at normal doses, but don't exceed 2 g/day; stricter for cirrhosis
- **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, etc.) — caution in cirrhosis, can cause kidney injury
- **Antibiotics** — macrolides, antifungals can be hepatotoxic
- **Statins** (cholesterol-lowering) — **generally safe**, check liver function regularly
- **Traditional Chinese medicine** — **the highest-risk category**, use with caution

**Daily avoidance:**
- Don't randomly take combo cold medicines
- Don't take random supplements to "protect the liver"
- **Before any doctor prescribes something, tell them you have chronic HBV**

---

## Managing comorbidities

Chronic HBV patients commonly have several comorbidities:

**① Fatty liver (MAFLD)**
- The most common comorbidity in modern patients
- Accelerates fibrosis and liver cancer
- 5–10% weight loss can improve ALT

**② Diabetes**
- HBV + diabetes = 3–4x HCC risk
- Glucose control = liver protection

**③ High cholesterol**
- TAF users need to pay closer attention
- Statins are relatively safe

**④ Hypertension**
- Most standard blood pressure drugs are safe
- Avoid meds that worsen liver injury

**⑤ Co-infection with HCV / HDV / HIV**
- Needs specialist assessment, treatment regimens are completely different

## Psychological angle: allow yourself to be "normal"

Many patients live in long-term anxiety after diagnosis:

- Am I going to get liver cancer any moment?
- Can I get married?
- Can I have children?
- Will my kids inherit it?

**Detailed answers are in Appendix E FAQ. Here, just one line:**

> **You're a person living with the virus, not a person defined by the virus.**

- Take meds on schedule, check up on schedule
- Work when you should work, love when you should love, exercise when you should exercise
- **Don't let the disease swallow your life**

Mental health matters just as much as liver health. If you need it, talk to a counselor.

---

## The family's role

A stable support system can change patient outcomes.

**What family can do:**
- Help remember checkup times
- Eat healthy together
- Exercise together
- **Treat you without prejudice** — no need for separate bowls and chopsticks
- Emotional support

**What family should do for themselves:**
- Complete HBV vaccination
- Check Anti-HBs to confirm protective antibodies
- Ordinary daily contact **doesn't transmit** — see Appendix E FAQ

---

## 📍 Key Points

- Alcohol, fatty liver, sketchy supplements — **the three most overlooked killers**
- No need to avoid foods, but **avoid fatty liver + aflatoxin**
- Exercise when you can, except during an acute flare
- **Complete your vaccinations** (hep A, flu, pneumonia, HPV, shingles)
- Before any new medication, tell the doctor you have chronic HBV
- Fatty liver / diabetes / high cholesterol comorbidities — manage them together
- **Mental health = half of liver health**
