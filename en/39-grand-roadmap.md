## The skeleton of the book: answers to seven questions

**What (Part 1) — What is hepatitis B?**

A DNA virus compressed by evolution to the extreme (3.2 kb, 7 proteins), which uses cccDNA to build a "viral hard drive" and integrated DNA to run an "HBsAg factory", letting it tug-of-war with humans for 60 years. 250 million carriers worldwide.

**Why (Part 2) — Why is it so hard to treat?**

Immune tolerance (playing dead) + T cell exhaustion (tired out) + cccDNA (hard drive) + integrated DNA (sleeper agent) — four walls stacked together. By comparison, HCV is simple (curable), HIV is medium (controllable), HBV is the hardest.

**Diagnosis (Part 3) — How do you understand yourself?**

Look at three sets of indicators: the "two-and-a-half pairs", viral quantification, and liver function. Plus fibrosis (FibroScan/FIB-4) and imaging (ultrasound/MRI). Different guidelines disagree on "when to treat", so you need to be able to judge for yourself.

**How (Part 4) — How is it treated today?**

Nucleoside drugs (ETV/TDF/TAF) + interferon (Peg-IFN) = the current main force. Suppressing the virus works. Clearing it basically doesn't. Functional cure rate is 0.3-10%, and most patients need long-term meds.

**New Research (Part 5) — What are the new drugs doing?**

siRNA + ASO + Capsid + Entry + therapeutic vaccine + immunotherapy + CRISPR. Combination therapy is the consensus. GSK's Bepirovirsen has hit a 20% functional cure rate and may be approved in 2027-2028.

**Future (Part 6) — What about the next 10 years?**

Functional cure rate goes from 20% to 50%. Combination regimens become mainstream. AI accelerates R&D. Personalized treatment becomes common. Sterilizing cure (CRISPR) is a 15-20 year long-term goal. WHO's 2030 elimination target is partially achievable.

**Summary (Part 7) — The patient's methodology?**

Know how to read papers, evaluate new drugs, spot fake cures, and use communities. Have the ability to judge for yourself. Don't be anxious, don't follow blindly.
