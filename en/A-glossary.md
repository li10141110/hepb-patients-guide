# Appendix A · HBV Terms and Abbreviations

> Alphabetical order, English glossary.

---

## A

**AASLD** — American Association for the Study of Liver Diseases
**AFP** — Alpha-Fetoprotein, a liver cancer marker
**ALT** — Alanine Aminotransferase, a sensitive indicator of liver injury
**Anti-HBc** — Antibody to HBcAg, hepatitis B core antibody
**Anti-HBe** — Antibody to HBeAg, hepatitis B e antibody
**Anti-HBs** — Antibody to HBsAg, hepatitis B surface antibody, protective antibody
**APASL** — Asian Pacific Association for the Study of the Liver
**APRI** — AST to Platelet Ratio Index, a serological score for liver fibrosis
**ARCUS** — Precision BioSciences' gene editing system
**ART** — Antiretroviral Therapy (HIV)
**ASO** — Antisense Oligonucleotide
**AST** — Aspartate Aminotransferase

## B

**Bepirovirsen** — GSK's ASO-class investigational drug
**Bulevirtide** — An approved Entry Inhibitor (EU)

## C

**CAM** — Capsid Assembly Modulator
**cccDNA** — Covalently Closed Circular DNA
**CHB** — Chronic Hepatitis B
**CRISPR** — Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats, a gene editing technology

## D

**DAA** — Direct-Acting Antiviral (an HCV term)
**Dane particle** — The complete hepatitis B virus particle
**DNA** — Deoxyribonucleic Acid

## E

**EASL** — European Association for the Study of the Liver
**ETV** — Entecavir
**EMA** — European Medicines Agency

## F

**FDA** — U.S. Food and Drug Administration
**FIB-4** — A serological score for liver fibrosis, based on age + AST + ALT + platelets
**FibroScan** — Transient elastography, a non-invasive assessment of liver fibrosis

## G

**GalNAc** — N-Acetyl Galactosamine, a liver-targeting delivery moiety
**Genotype** — HBV is divided into 10 genotypes, A through J

## H

**HBcAg** — Hepatitis B core Antigen
**HBcrAg** — Hepatitis B core-related Antigen
**HBeAg** — Hepatitis B e Antigen
**HBsAg** — Hepatitis B surface Antigen
**HBV** — Hepatitis B Virus
**HBV DNA** — Viral load
**HBV RNA** — Serum viral RNA, an indicator of cccDNA transcriptional activity
**HCC** — Hepatocellular Carcinoma
**HDV** — Hepatitis D Virus
**HIV** — Human Immunodeficiency Virus

## I

**IFN** — Interferon
**Integration** — Integration of HBV DNA into the host genome

## J

**JNJ-3989** — Arrowhead/Janssen's siRNA (also known as ARO-HBV)

## L

**LAM** — Lamivudine (first-generation nucleoside analog)
**LNP** — Lipid Nanoparticle, a delivery vehicle

## M

**METAVIR** — A liver fibrosis grading system (F0-F4)
**MRI** — Magnetic Resonance Imaging
**mRNA** — messenger RNA

## N

**NA / NUCs** — Nucleos(t)ide Analogs
**NHEJ** — Non-Homologous End Joining (a DNA repair pathway)
**NMPA** — National Medical Products Administration (China's drug regulator)
**NK** — Natural Killer Cell
**NTCP** — Sodium Taurocholate Cotransporting Polypeptide, the HBV entry receptor

## P

**PD-1** — Programmed Death 1, an immune checkpoint molecule
**Peg-IFN** — Pegylated Interferon
**pgRNA** — Pregenomic RNA
**PIVKA-II** — A liver cancer marker, also called DCP

## R

**rcDNA** — Relaxed Circular DNA (an HBV replication intermediate)
**RNAi** — RNA Interference
**RNase H** — The enzyme that cleaves RNA-DNA hybrid strands
**RCT** — Randomized Controlled Trial

## S

**siRNA** — Small Interfering RNA
**Sofosbuvir** — HCV cure drug (DAA)
**Sterilizing Cure** — Complete cure, virological eradication

## T

**T Cell Exhaustion** — Exhaustion of T cells
**TAF** — Tenofovir Alafenamide
**TDF** — Tenofovir Disoproxil Fumarate
**TDP2** — Tyrosyl-DNA Phosphodiesterase 2, an enzyme involved in cccDNA formation
**TLR** — Toll-like Receptor, an innate immunity sensor

## U

**ULN** — Upper Limit of Normal

## V

**VIR-2218** — Vir Biotechnology's siRNA
**VIR-3434** — Vir Biotechnology's anti-HBsAg monoclonal antibody
**VTP-300** — Vaccitech's (now Barinthus) therapeutic vaccine

## W

**WHO** — World Health Organization
