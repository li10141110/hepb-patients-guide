# Ch 18 · What is "functional cure"

> One word, argued over for 20 years.

---

## Three kinds of "cure"

The word "cure" has three levels in the HBV field.

**① Virological cure = complete clearance**
- cccDNA fully cleared
- Integrated DNA silenced or unexpressed
- HBV fully eradicated from the body

**Not achievable today, this is a long-term aspiration.**

**② Functional cure**
- HBsAg **persistently negative**
- Anti-HBs appears (not required, but better)
- HBV DNA **persistently undetectable**
- **No rebound after stopping**
- cccDNA still present, but "silenced"

**This is today's realistic goal, and the core endpoint of every new-drug trial.**

**③ Partial cure**
- Long-term HBV DNA suppression
- HBeAg seroconversion
- HBsAg still positive
- Requires long-term medication

**This is the standard endpoint for NUCs.**

---

## Why it's called "functional"

Because it doesn't chase eliminating the virus, only stops the virus from **functionally "expressing"**.

- Virus is still there (cccDNA still in the hepatocyte nucleus)
- Integrated DNA is still there
- But both are suppressed to a level **the immune system can keep in check**
- Like a tamed "sleeping lion"

**Upside of this definition**: realistic and achievable, doable within a finite course.
**Cost of this definition**: you have to accept that HBV-related **liver cancer risk isn't zero**.

---

## The official version of the definition

**AASLD / EASL 2019 consensus**:

> **Functional cure** =
> **Persistent HBsAg negativity + HBV DNA undetectable + normal ALT + 6+ months off treatment**

Some guidelines are stricter and require Anti-HBs seroconversion.

---

## Why it's an important goal

Patients who reach functional cure:

- **No lifelong medication needed** — less burden of compliance and cost
- **Liver cancer risk significantly reduced** — but not zero
- **No long-term rebound after stopping** — quality of life near normal
- **Psychological load massively lightened**

Studies show that in patients reaching functional cure:
- Annual HCC incidence **drops to 0.05–0.2%** (vs 1–3% untreated)
- Cirrhosis progression basically halts

**This is why the entire HBV new-drug field has taken "functional cure" as the consensus endpoint.**

---

## How to reach it today

**Spontaneous** — very rare (< 0.5%/year)

**Long-term NUCs** — **0.3–1% per year**, mostly in patients with low baseline HBsAg

**Peg-IFN 48 weeks** — **3–10%**, mostly in the high-response profile

**NUCs → Peg-IFN switch** — some studies show **10–15%**

**New-drug combinations (clinical trials)** — **20–30%+** (early data)

---

## The significance of GSK's 2026 news

In 2026, GSK released pivotal Phase 3 data for Bepirovirsen (ASO):

> **1 in 5 (about 20%) of patients reached functional cure**

This data is historic — **if approved, it will be** the first approved drug explicitly aimed at functional cure**.

Details:
- Bepirovirsen as monotherapy, 24-week injection course
- HBsAg loss rate of about 20% in the high-dose arm
- Sustained for 24–48 weeks after stopping
- Some patients had Anti-HBs seroconversion

**This is the concrete basis for saying "the 2020s are the pivot from 'control' to 'cure.'"**

---

## What to do after reaching functional cure

Even if you get there, you're not "completely well."

**You must continue:**

- **Recheck every 6 months** — at least the first 2 years
- **Abdominal ultrasound + AFP every 6–12 months**
- **HBsAg quantitation recheck** (small chance of rebound)
- **Watch out for immunosuppression** — chemotherapy, organ transplantation, biologics, etc., can reactivate the virus

**HBV reactivation risk:**

- Chemotherapy and B-cell depleters (rituximab, etc.) are high risk
- Even with HBsAg already negative and Anti-HBs present
- **Prophylactic antivirals may be needed**

**HCC surveillance can't stop:**

- Genomic damage caused by prior integrated DNA is permanent
- Especially in high-risk groups (men > 40, family history, prior cirrhosis)
- **At least yearly high-quality imaging is recommended**

---

## A psychological angle

When patients get the "functional cure" report, two common reactions:

**A. Huge relief**
"Finally free."

**B. Cautious skepticism**
"Is it real? Will it rebound?"

Both are reasonable. **The rational stance is "celebrate + stay cautious"**:
- Let yourself be happy
- But keep the monitoring cadence
- Don't do high-risk stuff (alcohol, sketchy supplements)
- Keep an annual full checkup

**From this moment, you shift from "patient" to "recovered." Mindset can change, monitoring cannot.**

---

## An open question: when can cccDNA actually be cleared

After functional cure, cccDNA is still there. **Can it be naturally used up?**

- No definite answer yet
- Mathematical models estimate **decades** for natural clearance
- Only younger patients with a stronger immune system might speed this up
- CRISPR or future direct cccDNA drugs might make "precise clearance" possible

**Functional cure is "cessation of hostility," not "the enemy is gone."**

---

## 📍 Key Points

- Three tiers of cure: virological eradication > functional cure > partial cure
- **Functional cure = persistent HBsAg negativity + undetectable DNA + no rebound after stopping**
- The **consensus endpoint** for all new drugs today
- Peg-IFN achieves it in 3–10%, new-drug trials 20–30%+
- **After reaching it, still need regular monitoring, especially for liver cancer**
- Can reactivate under immunosuppression
- cccDNA still present — **functional cure ≠ virological eradication**
